STRATEGIC INITIATIVES

UNICEF initiative - “Child and youth friendly community”

Since the end of 2018, Lviv has been participating in the UNICEF Child and Youth Friendly Community Initiative. As of December 2021, it has the official status of a “Child and Youth Friendly Community” by the decision of the commission.

"Child and Youth Friendly Community” - it’s a UNICEF initiative aimed at supporting local authorities in realizing children's rights.

The initiative has been implemented since 1996. The initiative has been supported by more than 3,000 municipalities in 38 countries on five continents. In Ukraine, the initiative has been implemented since 2018. More than 170 municipalities have expressed a desire to join the initiative. In Ukraine, as of August 1, 2021, 35 cities and communities (23 of which already have intermediate candidate status) with a population of more than 9 million people are participating in the initiative.

In November 2023, Lviv gained the status of the European Youth Capital 2025.

The European Youth Capital is a title awarded by the European Youth Forum, designed to empower young people, increase youth participation and strengthen European identity. Every year, a new European city gets the chance to showcase its innovative ideas, projects and activities aimed at raising the voice of young people and introducing a new youth perspective into all aspects of city life.

The title of the European Youth Capital is of huge value to the awarded city and includes a number of benefits at the local, regional and European levels.

Follow the implementation of the initiative using the hashtag #molofreedomguards

Youth well-being Index in Lviv was conducted twice: in 2019 and 2021.

The United Nations Population Fund measures the overall youth well-being index at the country level. This methodology has been adapted to the realities of Ukraine and a similar study has been launched at the municipal level.

In Lviv, the index was determined by the MoloDvizhCenter.Lviv and the City Institute. We need such a study to understand the current situation in the city, how youth-friendly the environment is and what can be done to change things for the better. The index will be useful for comparing the development of different cities.
